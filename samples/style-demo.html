The Ladder Problem Story

Maya is a firefighter. She parks her truck 5 meters from the base of a burning building. The window she needs to reach is 12 meters above the ground.

Her ladder runs from the truck to the window in a straight line — forming the longest side of a right triangle. How long must it be?

The Pythagorean Theorem gives us the answer instantly. No measuring tape needed.

Key idea: In any right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the two legs.

Step-by-Step Solution Math
1
Square each leg
a² = 25  ·  b² = 144
2
Add them together
a² + b² = 169
3
Take the square root — that's your hypotenuse
c = √169 = 13
Did you know?

The (3, 4, 5) triple is the oldest known Pythagorean triple — three whole numbers that perfectly satisfy the theorem. Ancient Egyptian builders used knotted ropes in this ratio to create perfect right angles when constructing the pyramids. Set a = 3 and b = 4 to see c become exactly 5.
