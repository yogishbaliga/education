🎮 Grid Game

You're playing a two-player grid game with Kai. On their turn they announced: "My piece is at position (−3, 2)."

Below is your game grid. Can you find their piece?

The game grid Act 1 · Mystery
A point is hidden on this grid. The announcement said "(−3, 2)". What information would help you find it?
Think about it: you need to know which direction the numbers count, and where zero is.
Move to Act 2 to get that information.
Request information Act 2 · Gather clues

Each button reveals one piece of information. Collect what you need to find the piece at (−3, 2).

The origin (0, 0) is the center of the grid — the intersection of the two main lines. All distances are measured from here.
The first number tells you to move left or right. Positive = right. Negative = left. −3 means 3 steps left.
The second number tells you to move up or down. Positive = up. Negative = down. +2 means 2 steps up.

🎯 Found it!

Starting at origin (0, 0) → move 3 left (−3) → move 2 up (+2) → that's the piece. And now you know how coordinates work.

The formal definition Act 3 · Concept
Ordered pair (x, y) — two numbers that name one exact point on a coordinate plane.
• The x-value (first) tells left (+) or right (−) distance from origin.
• The y-value (second) tells up (+) or down (−) distance from origin.
• Order matters: (−3, 2) ≠ (2, −3).
