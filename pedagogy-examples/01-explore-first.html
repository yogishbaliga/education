What you just discovered Concept

Every point on the grid has two numbers that pinpoint it exactly. We write them as (x, y) — called coordinates or an ordered pair.

First number (x): how far to move right (positive) or left (negative) from the center.
Second number (y): how far to move up (positive) or down (negative) from the center.
The center is called the origin, written as (0, 0).

Notice that the order matters — (3, −2) is a different spot than (−2, 3). That's why they're called ordered pairs.

Real-world connection

GPS on your phone works the same way — every location on Earth has two coordinates: latitude (up/down, the y) and longitude (left/right, the x). The (0, 0) origin is in the Gulf of Guinea off the west coast of Africa, where the Equator meets the Prime Meridian.
