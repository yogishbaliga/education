The Map Becomes a Grid Pictorial

The city map transformed into a coordinate plane. East → right (+x). West → left (−x). North → up (+y). South → down (−y). The Town Square became the origin (0, 0).

Kai was at 3E, 2N → on the grid that's (3, 2)
Riley was at 1W, 4N → on the grid that's (−1, 4)

The direction names changed, but the locations are identical. The grid is just a more precise way to describe the same thing.

Writing Coordinates Abstract

We write coordinates as (x, y). The x-value comes first (left/right), the y-value second (up/down). These are called ordered pairs because the order matters.

(3, 2) → 3 right, 2 up
(−1, 4) → 1 left, 4 up
(−3, −2) → 3 left, 2 down
(4, −1) → 4 right, 1 down
