Approach 2 — Direct Instruction (Define → Model → Practice)
By the end of this lesson you will be able to plot any ordered pair (x, y) on a coordinate plane.
Key vocabulary Definitions
Coordinate plane
A flat grid made by two number lines crossing at right angles.
Origin
The center point (0, 0) where the two axes cross.
x-axis
The horizontal number line. Positive goes right, negative goes left.
y-axis
The vertical number line. Positive goes up, negative goes down.
Ordered pair
Two numbers written as (x, y) that name one exact point.
Quadrants
The four regions of the plane, numbered I–IV counterclockwise.
The coordinate plane: • Right (+x) and Up (+y) are positive.
• Left (−x) and Down (−y) are negative.
• Any point can be reached from the origin using exactly two moves.

Remember: x always comes first.
